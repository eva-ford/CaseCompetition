New York Times Best Selling Author

Suzanne Collins is an American author known for her young adult fiction, particularly the Hunger Games trilogy, which has become a cultural phenomenon. Born in 1962 in Connecticut, Collins began her career as a writer for children's television shows, including "Clarissa Explains It All" and "The Mystery Files of Shelby Woo."

In 2003, she published her first novel, "Gregor the Overlander," which was the first in a five-book series. However, it was the publication of "The Hunger Games" in 2008 that brought her widespread fame and success. The novel was a bestseller and spawned two sequels, "Catching Fire" and "Mockingjay," which were also highly successful.

In addition to her work as a writer, Collins has also been an advocate for various causes, including childhood hunger and the importance of reading. She has received numerous awards for her writing, including the California Young Reader Medal, the Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Award, and the American Library Association's Margaret A. Edwards Award..
